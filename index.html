Why Maydi?

Boswellia frereana, known locally as Maydi, grows only in the rugged mountains of northern Somaliland, where the dry winds from the Gulf of Aden meet the red desert soil. This unique micro-climate cannot be replicated anywhere else, giving the resin its distinctive aroma and purity.

Unlike other frankincense species, Maydi has a sweet citrus-honey scent and contains natural anti-inflammatory compounds valued in both traditional medicine and modern wellness. It’s used to ease stress, improve focus, support skin health, and enhance breathing via natural aromatherapy.

What is Frankincense (Olibanum)?

Frankincense (olibanum) is an aromatic resin obtained from trees of the genus Boswellia. Maydi (Boswellia frereana) from Somaliland is prized for its naturally sweet, citrus profile and clean burn. It’s used in incense, perfumery and wellness—often preferred when a bright, honey-citrus note is desired.
